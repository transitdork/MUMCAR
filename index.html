Why it ranks first

MG S5 EV ▶

Essence 49

~$44k driveaway (est.)
  • 49 kWh LFP · 335 km · 125 kW
  • Full Essence kit: power tailgate w/ foot-kick, pano sunroof, heated seats & wheel, wireless charging, 360° camera

Essence 62

$47–49k driveaway
  • 62 kWh LFP · 450 km brochure (some press: 425 km) · 150 kW
  • Same equipment, just more range & power — the one to get
Why it ranks second
Nothing else gives this much standard equipment for under $50k. LFP battery = charge to 100% every night without a second thought. Physical climate buttons and a simple layout — reviewers literally praise it for being “easy”. Warranty can stretch to 10 years/250,000 km if serviced at MG.
What to weigh up
Home AC charging is 6.6 kW (fine overnight, just the slowest here). No head-up display, no frunk. iSMART app is free for only 12 months. Cheaper “Excite” grades exist but drop the tailgate/sunroof kit.

A note on charging speed in these scores. Because charging will happen at home on a single-phase Type 2 wallbox, DC fast-charging speed was given very little weight. On a typical 7 kW home charger, all six cars charge at effectively the same speed (~6.6–7 kW — the car's on-board charger or the wallbox is the limit, whichever is lower). The Atto 3's slower DC charging therefore only matters on long road trips, and its Range & Charging score reflects that (7.5, not the 6.5 a road-tripper would give it).

“What if the Atto 3 Evo weren't coming?” The Evo costs the current Atto 3 points in two places: Value (buying a model months before replacement typically means a resale-value hit and paying full price for outgoing tech) and Ownership (the outgoing model's long-term software/parts priority is less certain). Remove the Evo from the picture and those recover to Value 9.0 and Ownership 7.0, giving a weighted total of 7.7 — essentially level with the Kona Electric, but still behind the EV3 and S5. That's because the Atto 3's biggest remaining deduction is unrelated to the Evo: its Everyday Ease score (6.5) reflects a screen-heavy control layout, plus the fine-print component warranties. In short: yes, the Evo drags the score down by about 0.2, but it isn't why the Atto 3 sits fourth.
