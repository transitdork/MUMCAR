The B10 is the tightest overall fit — longest (4,515 mm, 25 mm to spare) and widest. It’s a superb car and still fits, but it leaves the least room, so it’s the one to physically measure against before deciding.

One practical tip: measure the garage’s usable depth with the door closed, rather than trusting the CX-5 as the yardstick. Rear overhangs and tailgate shapes differ, and you’ll want room to walk past the boot. If the CX-5 fits with a few centimetres to spare, everything here fits with more.

How the scores were calculated

Each car is marked out of 10 in six categories, weighted for a driver who mostly does local trips, values ease and safety, and charges at home. Because you told me fast public charging isn’t a concern, DC charging speed carries almost no weight — on a home Type 2 wall box every car here charges at a similar rate.

Range & home charging — 15%WLTP range, Type 2 speed, and whether daily 100% charging is fine (LFP).
Ownership — 12%Warranty length and clarity, servicing cost, dealer network.
Practicality — 8%Boot, frunk, tailgate, cabin storage, rear-seat flexibility.
